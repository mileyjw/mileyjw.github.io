My Start In Media

I wanted to share some of the past work I have done and why it has given me so much joy!

I have loved graphic design for a long time, but my actual start point of creating work was in high school.

My last semester of my senior year was the first time in school history that we got to have a graphic design class.

That year I took initiative and started to create graphics for our sports teams...

Before that, two things had stopped me from creating graphics in the past:

  • I didn't have the time to go to all the sporting events and take photos.
  • My high school self did not want to be the "weirdo" taking unauthorized photos of my peers.

So, yearbook class gave me the authority and inspiration to start...

Reflecting back has made me realize how significant this experience was for me.

Track Meet Day Announcement Graphic

Details

So, for this graphic I used Canva. I got inspiration for this concept from other sports graphics. I thought it was cool how some part of the athlete extended the photo. I recreated this by first making two copies of the photo. Then i removed the background of one of the photos so the athlete was the only subject. This allowed me to change the background into a color that would make the athlete stand out. I wanted to keep everything else pretty simple so I chose just two different fonts and keep the color black.

Games and Puzzles Logo

Details

For my graphic desing studio class we got to work with clients from our school who needed a brand refresh. I got to work with the office of sustainabilty and create work for the game and puzzles shelf.

I got to collaborate with two other people in my class. I had the idea of incorporating the St.Thomas sheild and I tied it to the program we were working with by adding game icons in the middle.

The singage was another aspect of the games and puzzles cliet I got to work with. My team and I split up our work and I got to create the signage that they are going to put up around their physical shelves.

I created the colorful border with the idea to look like a board game. I also had to explin to readers how to use the shelf in an effient way. After getting feedback from our client I added a QR code so readers would have a easy way to get to their website
