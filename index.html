my start in media

I wanted to share some of the past work I have done and why it has given me so much joy! I have loved graphic design for a long time, but my actual start point of creating work was in high school. My last semester of my senior year was the first time in school history that we got to have a graphic design class. That year I took initiative and started to create graphics for our sports teams. No one asked me to, but I thought it would create more excitement about sporting events for our athletes and students since no one else was doing it. Along with my media class, I was also taking a yearbook class. Within the yearbook class, we got to take photos of school events, including sports, so it allowed me to have access to tons of photos of all our athletes. Before that, two things had stopped me from creating graphics in the past: 1. I didn’t have the time to go to all the sporting events and take photos. 2. My high school self did not want to be the “weirdo” taking unauthorized photos of my peers. So, yearbook class gave me the authority and inspiration to start. I played softball, so that was my first dip in the deep waters. I created graphics of my teammates and just posted them on my Instagram story. I also thought it would get more people to our games. (It did not…lol) Even so, people were still seeing my work, and the feedback from my students, teachers, and coaches was the greatest part! Soon after, I got asked by the men’s basketball coach to make graphics for their game days and announcements. Once I started creating for basketball, I thought it would be a good idea to make an Instagram account and post my graphics on there for documentation. I knew it would also be a more accessible platform for the rest of the students at my school since the basketball coach was only posting them on X (formerly Twitter). Having to create a graphic for someone other than myself taught me a lot of things and made me be more intentional towards my work. It also allowed me to be freely creative. It really was the best of both worlds! Reflecting back has made me realize how significant this experience was for me. If I had not just decided to start creating things I loved just to do it, I may not be where I am today.
